Professional Wrestlers Who Sadly Passed Away At a Young Age

Professional wrestlers seem to die before the age of 55 than any other athlete. The abuse of their bodies, steroids, hard drugs, partying, long hours, and ultimately their deaths, are what end these hulking of the squared circle. Many of the greatest wrestlers of all time are not included. It’s almost like an occupational hazard to be a pro wrestler, considering all the bumps you get in the ring. As the sport of wrestling has grown in popularity and become a global brand of entertainment, the deaths of these wrestling giants have become more frequent. Many of the stars on this list have competed in professional wrestling. The men and women on this list have worked in some of the biggest arenas and pay-per-view events in the world.

So which wrestlers died young? That’s the shame of this list. There are guys like Owen Hart, who died during an in-ring stunt, or the tragedy of Chris Benoit and his family, which words can’t really do justice. Others, like Andre The Giant was a victim of his own size and ultimately that size was one of the reasons for his great success.

The wrestlers died before their 55th birthday. Take a look at the list of dead wrestlers and see who died young.

 

1. Chyna (1969-2016)

In April 2016 a beloved female wrestler was found dead in her home. Police were unsure as to the cause of the death of Chyna, who was 45 when she died. When she won the Women’s Championship in 1996, she became the first woman to win a professional wrestling title.

She was the only female champion in WWF history. She appeared in music videos and adult films after her success in the ring.

 

2. The Ultimate Warrior (1959-2014)

Warrior passed away in Arizona the day after he appeared on Raw for the first time since 1996 and gave an emotional speech after being in the WWE Hall of Fame. His wife, Dana, and their two daughters, Indiana and Mattigan, are his survivors.

 

3. Randy Savage (1952-2011)

Lynn was the passenger in Macho Man’s Jeep when he died of a sudden heart attack. The cause of Macho Man’s death was ruled to be heart disease, a condition that he was unaware of and was not being treated for.

 

4. Chris Benoit (1967-2007)

There is a tragedy that is still taboo in wrestling circles. Chris Benoit killed his wife and son over a three-day period before taking his own life. It was found that concussions had caused significant damage and lead to dementia, which can cause behavioral problems, after an examination of Chris Benoit’s brain.

When WWE learned of the sudden passing of the wrestler, the company aired a tribute, only to bury it once they learned the full story.

 

5. Miss Elizabeth (1960-2003)

Miss Elizabeth passed away in Georgia. The person called the emergency number. She died at the hospital. The cause of “acute toxicity” from a mixture of pain killers and vodka is officially listed.

 

6. Eddie Guerrero (1967-2005)

His nephew, a long-time tag team partner, found him unresponsive in his Minneapolis, Minnesota hotel room. Eddie died when the paramedics arrived on the scene. Acute heart failure was the cause of death. He was posthumously accepted into the Hall of Fame.

 

7. Mr. Perfect (1958-2003)

8. Owen Hart (1965-1999)

Owen fell to his death at the ‘Over The Edge’ WWE Pay Per View event while being lowered to the ring in a harness via a grapple line. Hart fell 78 feet and was thrown into the ring. The death was caused by internal bleeding from the fall. One of the worst tragedies in wrestling history.
