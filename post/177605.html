52-Year-Old Gillian Anderson Is Done Wearing Bras: 'I Don't Care If My Breasts Reach My Belly Button'

No matter how loud we say that others’ opinions don’t bother us, it does. We are always thinking about what others think about us. We always want to know how our family sees us. And we are interested in knowing whether or not our friends like us.

But do you know what? As we mature, we get free from these thoughts. As we age, we don’t care about what society says is “normal” or socially acceptable. That’s because aging not just turns our hair grey, but also makes us wiser.

And Gillian Anderson is proving this thing. That’s why she has said no to wearing a bra. She doesn’t care if her breasts reach her belly button, she is done with wearing a bra.

Continue reading to get into the details of this story.

Bras have long since been a symbol of contention for women.

Did you know when the bra came into the market? Well, it was around the 20th century, when the world saw bras. But women have been binding and supporting their breasts since Ancient Greece. 

These days, it’s becoming more and more common for women to “free the nipple” on a daily basis.

Of course, it started out as a trend but now, it’s becoming more of a lifestyle. A movement if you will.

The latest celebrity to announce they’re going braless forever is esteemed actress Gillian Anderson.

I know, right? The 6 time Emmy winner may be onto something…

Gillian’s career dates back to the ’90s.

Known best for her role on the popular TV show The X-Files and now, Netflix’s The Crown, Gillian is no stranger to hard work.

And you know what hard-working are tired of? Wearing bras!

Due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, many women got used to not wearing uncomfortable breast shackles every day.

Now that lockdowns have lifted in Britain, the actress is determined to keep this one piece of pandemic life with her.

In a new video posted to her Instagram account, the 52-year-old revealed she was done with wearing bras.

To be honest, who can blame her?

“I don’t wear a bra anymore. I can’t wear a bra. I’m sorry,” she revealed during an Instagram live session, as per *Page Six.*

“I don’t care if my breasts reach my belly button. I’m not wearing a bra, it’s just too [explective] uncomfortable,” she added.
