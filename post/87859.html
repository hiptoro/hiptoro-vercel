Couple moves to Thai Island and adopts 15 strays after losing their Doggo

We all have a soft corner for our little canine friends in the corner of our hearts. And that is why we try to do all the possible activities to show our love for them. Also, the little doggos become a special part of our families, once we adopt them. And we strive hard to fill the vacant space that is created once they are gone. A couple too after losing their beloved 10-year-old dog has experienced the same feeling. Thus, in the remembrance of their little pet, they have adopted 15 strays.

This couple has become the best friend of stray dogs living in Koh Kood Island. They moved to Thailand six years ago. And since then, they are taking good care of these little dogs. The Lithuanian couple, Mantas and Rasa drive 40 km every day to feed these strays, give them medicines, and do regular checkups. As there are no vets on the island, they have to take extra care of the stray dogs.

We know that after knowing a gist, you might be interested to know more about their story. So, we have curated their whole story below. Scroll down and have fun!

#1 Mantas and Rasa came to Kooh Kod Island 6 years ago as tourists

#2 The couple quickly fall in love with the island. It was actually something they were looking for.

#3 They had to say goodbye to their little boxer, Aibo via phone as they were staying in Koh Kood Island

#4 Aibo left the couple devastated

#5 Couple started to come to know about how many dogs were there on the island

#6 Many strays were sick or injured but there were no animal shelters or vets to cure them

#7 The couple decided to adopt their first pup, named Zuika

#8 They decided to stay in Thailand & went for a short trip back to Europe to bring their other dog named Rudis with them

#9 Mantas and Rasa have adopted 15 strays since they began to live in Thailand

#10 Two doggos, out of them are blind and four are suffering from other disabilities

#11 The duo constantly contacts the vets from their home country, Lithuania as there are no animal shelters or pet sanctuaries on Kooh Kod Island

#12 Vets from abroad donate their knowledge, food, and medicines for these strays

#13 The couple drives 40 km a day to feed stray dogs and provide medications

#14 Back in 2015, they started a project to control the dog’s population on the island

#15 Mantas and Rasa are trying their best to take care of these strays living on the island

No doubt, that after looking at what this amazing couple is up to, we can surely say that humanity is still there around us. Also, if all of us do our bit to take care of the little animals around us, we will surely be able to make this world a better place.
